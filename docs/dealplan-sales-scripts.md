# DealPlan Sales Scripts — Free → Paid Conversion

## Context
These scripts drive the GTM funnel (`docs/dealplan-gtm-funnel.md`): a business is
already on the calendar via a **free aggregated listing**, and the rep's job is to
move them up to a **paid resident-exclusive deal**. The spine of every script is
the **measurement gap**:

> A free listing creates impressions but can't prove who walked in. The only way
> to *know* what Leaf is worth is an exclusive deal that redeems on verified
> location. **That proof is what they're buying.**

## A. The warm opener (post-claim or after a listing report)
Use right after a business claims their listing, or alongside the monthly report.

> "Good news — your [happy hour] is already on the calendar in **[6]** buildings
> near you and got **[400]** views from residents last month. Here's the catch:
> that's all I can tell you. A free listing shows you impressions, but I can't
> prove a single one of those people actually walked in — they might've come
> anyway.
>
> If you want to *know*, add one **resident-exclusive deal** — something only
> neighbors on the calendar get. Then I show you exactly who scheduled, who
> showed up, and who was brand-new — verified by location at your counter. You'll
> finally see what we're actually worth to you. Want to set one up? It's free to
> start — you only pay when someone you'd never have met walks in."

---

## B. The core upsell script (generic, any vertical)
1. **Anchor on their data:** "[400] impressions, [6] buildings, last month — free."
2. **Open the gap:** "But impressions aren't customers, and I can't prove walk-ins
   from a listing."
3. **Offer the bridge:** "An exclusive deal closes that — verified redemption,
   real names, new-vs-regular, all measured."
4. **Add the social multiplier:** "And because a neighbor can invite the people in
   their building, one booking often becomes a group — bigger ticket, not a single
   coupon."
5. **Kill the risk:** "Start on performance — $0 up front, you pay only on a
   verified walk-in. Worst case it costs you nothing."
6. **Close:** "Let's turn on one deal for your slowest day and look at the numbers
   in two weeks."

## C. Vertical variants (swap the hook to match the business model)

### Coffee shop — *frequency play*
- **Hook:** "You don't need a one-time crowd, you need **regulars**. The people in
  the buildings around you could be in every morning."
- **Math:** "One neighbor on a $5 drink three mornings a week is **$60/month** —
  and they bring the habit, not a coupon. Two of those and the deal's paid for many
  times over."
- **Deal shape:** small standing perk ("free pastry with any drink, neighbors
  only") that builds a routine, not a discount that trains people to wait for deals.

### Salon / beauty — *high-ticket, new-regular value*
- **Hook:** "A new client isn't a haircut — it's a year of haircuts. What's a
  regular worth to you over 12 months? **$500+?**"
- **Math:** "On performance you pay ~$8 for a *verified new* client worth hundreds.
  That's the cheapest acquisition you'll ever run."
- **Deal shape:** "20% off your first visit, residents only" — gated to genuinely
  new customers, so you're buying acquisition, not discounting your regulars.

### Bar / restaurant happy hour — *fill the slow nights*
- **Hook:** "Your happy hour's already listed — let's point it at your **dead
  Tuesday**, not the nights you're already full."
- **Math:** "A four-top of neighbors who scheduled together is one table you didn't
  have. The social mechanic turns one RSVP into a group."
- **Deal shape:** day-parted exclusive ("neighbors: $2 off, Tue–Wed only") +
  open-to-company so residents pull a crew.

### Fitness / yoga — *trial-to-membership*
- **Hook:** "Your hardest sale is the first class. The buildings around you are
  full of people who'd try if a neighbor went with them."
- **Math:** "One converted member is recurring revenue for months — a verified
  first-class redemption is a lead you can prove."
- **Deal shape:** "First class free for residents," social so friends come
  together (higher trial-to-join rate).

## D. Objection handling
- **"Isn't this just Groupon?"** → "Opposite. Groupon ships you deal-hunters from
  across the city who use it once. Every person here lives **next door** and can
  become a regular. And you pay on *verified* walk-ins, not impressions."
- **"How do I know I'm not paying for coupon screenshots?"** → "You're not. The
  discount won't activate unless the person is **physically in your shop** during
  the window — GPS + time verified. No location, no charge."
- **"I already advertise on Instagram/Yelp."** → "Those reach strangers and you pay
  whether anyone shows or not. This reaches your nearest neighbors and on
  performance you pay **only when one walks in.**"
- **"I don't have time to manage this."** → "You don't. We set the deal once. You
  glance at a 'Redeemed ✓' screen at the register — that's it."
- **"Why pay when the free listing already shows my happy hour?"** → "Because the
  free listing can't prove it sent you anyone. You're paying for **proof and a
  private, measurable channel** — and the social mechanic that turns one booking
  into a group."
- **"$99/month is a lot."** → "Let's not start there. Start on performance at $0 —
  pay only on verified walk-ins. Once you've seen the volume, a flat fee is often
  *cheaper* than the per-walk-in fees, and we'll switch you when the math says so."

## F. The conversion ladder (which script, when)
- **Listed → Claim:** "you're getting seen / your info's wrong — fix it free"
  (gets them identified and logged in).
- **Claim → Exclusive:** scripts A/B above, at the end of the claim flow and in the
  monthly report (the measurement gap is the wedge).
- **Exclusive → Featured:** once redemptions are strong and a competitor is also on
  the building's calendar — "lock the category before they do" (Featured $99 /
  Exclusive $299).
